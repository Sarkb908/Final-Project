Being a Muslim American Woman

I was born in Elmhurst, Queens being the first generation American with parents who immigrated directly from Bangladesh. I grew up with a plethora of diversity around me, and my biggest divide always seemed to be the place I came from, and the place I grew up in. I have adapted to the American culture quite well; although my roots and characteristics come from what I was taught at home where Bengali traditions and religious Islamic practice was very well integrated throughout my life.

I’ve considered myself all of those things, because they are what make me who I am. I’ve learned a lot throughout my life, gained a lot of experience, and lost a lot by being the Muslim American Woman I am today. It hasn’t always been easy growing up with such a rigid divide in my own home, but I can say the experience has been unique. Growing up was a constant struggle because I faced many contradictions between the two cultures I grew up in, and the two lives I’ve tried to balance.
